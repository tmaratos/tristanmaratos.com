Standalone products, one operating standard

Four apps under one parent company standard—each built for a distinct job. Steward is the shared guide: clear rules, real data, not random chat.

Shared guide

Steward

Steward is not a fifth product—it is the discipline across CFO, Sentinel, Eden, and Ledger. It keeps answers tied to structured inputs, transparent rules, and what you actually supplied. No invented fields, no hand-wavy chat.

Flagship · Finance

CFO

The financial operating system

Financial OS on your machine, powered by Steward—not thin budgeting. Interpret, plan, and decide from structured data and transparent rules.

  • Money, plans, and projections—local-first where it matters.
  • Decisions grounded in your ledger and assumptions, not generic advice.
  • Not a research workspace or identity vault—CFO stays in finance.
Investigation · Evidence

Sentinel

Intelligence & evidence analysis

Investigative workspace for evidence review, claim tracing, and structured findings—Steward keeps conclusions tied to sources you actually supplied, not speculative narrative.

  • Evidence review, timelines, and claim comparison—not a finance dashboard.
  • Forensic-style rigor: alertness without noise, analysis without invention.
  • Not open-ended research chat or identity vault—Sentinel stays in investigation.
Research

Eden

Research intelligence and prioritization

Standalone research workspace: gather sources, compare claims, and manage citations. Steward helps prioritize what fits your question.

  • Sources, claims, and citations—not a finance dashboard.
  • Compare evidence side by side before you commit to a conclusion.
  • Steward narrows focus to material that answers the question you asked.
Identity & documents

Ledger

Secure vault. Exact-fill documents.

Encrypted identity vault and document assistant—for example, spouses sharing a household file. Steward lines up form fields with stored values; blanks stay blank if no data is supplied.

  • Secure vault plus exact-fill—no invented field values.
  • Household and identity documents with controlled sharing.
  • Not finance planning or open-ended research—Ledger protects what you store.
